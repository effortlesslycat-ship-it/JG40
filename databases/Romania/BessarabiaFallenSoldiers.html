Russian-Jewish Fallen Soldiers of WWI from Bessarabia

1,559 Records

Military Records · Casualties · Bessarabia

Background

This database contains information about 1,559 Jewish soldiers in the Russian army from Bessarabia Gubernia who were killed, wounded, or went missing during the First World War. The lists are published on the website of the Russian State Library, with images of more than 50,000 pages covering 1914–1916.

In 2014, the JewishGen Bessarabia Research Division learned of these lists’ availability. Under the coordination of Joel Ratner, various Jewish genealogical groups were invited to collaborate on extracting all information from the Russian-language lists. While lists for 1917 and 1918 are not currently available, it is hoped they will also be scanned.

Of the 1,559 entries (some soldiers appear more than once), 638 were listed as Missing in Action, 179 Killed in Action, 699 Wounded, and the rest with miscellaneous statuses. Hundreds of towns are represented, with the largest concentrations from Kishinev (250), Bieltsy (58), Bendery (53), Lipkany (48), and Brichany (45). Most held the rank of Private (1,266).

Fields Recorded
Name — Family name and given name(s)
Patronymic — Father’s given name
Rank — Military rank
Religion — “Jewish” or blank
Marital Status — Single, married, widower, or blank
Town — Town or village of origin (m. = town, s. = village)
Uyezd — District of origin
Status — Wounded, Missing, or Killed
Date — Date declared dead, wounded, or missing
Source Issue, Page — Issue and page number of the source listing
Obtain a copy: Any researcher may obtain a scan of the pages with their family entries by making a qualifying contribution of $18 or more to the Bessarabia Research Division’s General Fund.
