Surviving Jews in Bessarabia

1,782 Records

Holocaust · Survivors · Bessarabia · Moldova · Ukraine

Background

This database is derived from the book First List of Surviving Jews in Bessarabia (published by the Bessarabian Federation of American Jews, New York, February 1946), which contains information on 1,782 people from Bessarabia who survived the Holocaust and returned to a Bessarabian village.

The purpose of the book was to collect addresses of returning Bessarabian Jews so that packages of food and clothing could be sent to them. Not all towns were included, and it is not known how towns were selected. The town of Lipcani had three times more survivors than any other town and comprised over one-third of all names in the book. Additional names were drawn from a World Jewish Congress list (May 1946) and a YIVO emigre list (1945).

Fields Recorded
Name — Name of the survivor
Age — Age of the survivor
Father — Father’s name
Family Information — Given names of accompanying family; surnames in capitals indicate alternate/maiden names; “(orphan)” if applicable
Address — Street or postal address; “dovestrebovania” = “hold at Post Office”
Town — Current name of the town
Country — Current country (Moldova or Ukraine)
Document Town — Town name as spelled in the source
Source Page — BFAJ (main book), WJC (World Jewish Congress), or EMIGRE (YIVO list) + page number
