Background

The Reviskie Skazki (Revision Lists) were conducted in territories ruled by the Russian Czar in the 18th and 19th centuries. They enumerated individuals and families subject to taxation and identified men to draft into the army. There were ten major revisions across the Russian Empire; for Bessarabia, four may apply: the seventh (1815–1825), eighth (1833–1835), ninth (1848–1854), and tenth (1856–1859).

Bessarabia was ceded to the Russian Empire by the Treaty of Bucharest in May 1812. It is not clear whether the seventh revision was held there due to the region’s special status, and no Jewish records from the seventh or eighth revisions have been found. However, ninth-revision records contain former registration numbers from the eighth revision, confirming its existence.

This project of translating and transcribing Bessarabia Revision Lists began in August 2009, using records microfilmed by the LDS Family History Library. The database also includes Name Lists, Family Records, Alphabetical Special Lists, and Common Lists.

LDS Microfilms: The LDS microfilmed Revision Lists for all districts of Bessarabia in 2003 at the Moldova State Archives. There are 292 microfilms total, of which at least 30 contain confirmed Jewish records. A complete, continually updated list of microfilms and a complete inventory are available via Google Sheets.
Fields Recorded

The Revision Lists may contain the following information:

Page # — Page number of the image (usually two pages per image: left for men, right for women)
Registration # — Assigned family number for a particular town’s registry
Surname — Last name
Given Name — First name(s)
Father — Father’s given name (derived from the patronymic)
Relationship to Head of Household
Sex — M for male, F for female
Age
Age at Last Revision — Only present for men
Reason Left / Year Left
Comments — Family status (Merchant, Middle class, Farmer, military), registration date, and occasionally origin information
Former Registration # — Number in the previous Revision (sometimes two prior numbers)
Day, month, year of registration — Julian calendar
Uyezd — One of nine Bessarabian districts: Akkerman, Beltsy, Bendery, Izmail, Kagul, Khotin, Kishinev, Orgeev, Soroki
Gubernia — “Bessarabia”
Type of Record — Revision List, Additional Revision List, Alphabetical List, Special List, or Common List
Archive / Fond / Inventory / File — Archival citation at MoldSA
LDS Microfilm # — Microfilm number at the LDS Family History Library
Image Number
Research Notes
Julian Calendar

Dates are recorded according to the Julian calendar, which was used throughout the Russian Empire until 1918. To convert to the Gregorian (modern) calendar, add 12 days for dates between March 1800 and February 1900.

Kehila Records (1824)

Several record sets from 1824 are named “Kehila records,” found only in Khotin uezd. There were four different kehilot: Khotin, Lipkani, Brichani, and Ataki. For example, each person in the Lipkani kehila was registered in Lipkani but lived in one of 48 surrounding localities. Across all four kehilot, Jews lived in about 200 localities in Khotin uezd, with some villages having only 2–3 Jewish families.
