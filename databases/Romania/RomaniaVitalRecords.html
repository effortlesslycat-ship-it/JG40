Background

This database contains Jewish vital records from various regions of Romania. The majority of records come from the historical regions of Moldavia and Wallachia, including the largest communities of Iaşi and Bucharest, as well as many smaller towns.

The Romanian civil registration system began in the 1860s, before which records were kept by religious authorities. For much of Romania, pre-civil-registration records have not survived (with the exception of Transylvania, formerly part of the Hungarian Empire). Vital records are stored in Romania’s regional archives, one per county. They are generally not available online, and the LDS Church has not scanned the majority of present-day Romanian records.

Romanian vital records are civil records and held together for all religions. Each record is generally a full handwritten page (marriages may span several pages). Birth records contain date and place of birth, names of parents, ages, residence, and witnesses. Marriage records contain similar information for bride and groom. Death records generally do not include names of parents (except for the death of a child) and most do not identify a cause of death. All records will likely include original signatures.

Note: This collection does not include records from certain regions of present and former Romania, which are covered in separate databases: Bucovina, Maramureş, Bessarabia (Moldova), and parts of western Romania formerly part of Hungary.
Fields Recorded

The fields captured vary by record type. In general:

Births — Date and place of birth, parents’ names, ages and residence, witnesses
Marriages — Date and place, bride and groom information, parents’ names, ages, residence, witnesses
Deaths — Date and place of death, name of deceased; parents’ names generally only for deaths of children
All record types — Original signatures of parties and witnesses where present

A complete inventory of records by town and year is available via Google Sheets.
