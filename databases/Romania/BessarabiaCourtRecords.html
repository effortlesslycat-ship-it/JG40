Bessarabia Court Records

11,659 Records

Legal Records · Bessarabia · Moldova

Background

This database contains Jewish surnames referenced in court cases across various jurisdictions in Bessarabia, drawn from the inventories of the State Archive of Moldova and covering the years 1806 to 1944. These records serve as a valuable guide for future research in the holdings of the archive.

Chişinău (the capital of Moldova today) was known as Kishinev during the interwar period when it was part of Romania, and before that served as the capital of Bessarabia Gubernia in the Russian Empire.

Courts covered: Kishinev District Court, Kishinev City Court, Kishinev Court of Appeal, township and city courts, orphanage and justice of the peace records, district land courts (Beltsy, Yassy, Kagul, Lapushna, Lipkany, Soroki, Tighina, Orgeev, Bendery), private and public notary offices, district tribunals and courts during the Romanian occupation (1918–1944), police and quester inventories, and records from the People’s Courts and Supreme Court of the Moldavian SSR.
Fields Recorded

Each record contains the following fields:

Name — Surname and given name of individuals involved
Place of Residence — Town, Uezd, and Guberniya
Archive Information — Archive name, fond name, case number, and inventory page number
Court Information — Full name and location of the court
Case Description — A brief description of the case
Case Dates — Start and finish dates of the case

  • Property Deed Violation — A dispute over the property deed of Volf Groysman, a resident of Tighina.
