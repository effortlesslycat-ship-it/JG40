1901 Klyachkin All-Russia Business Directory

544 Records

Business Directory · Bessarabia · Transnistria

Background

This database contains 544 records of Jews listed in the 1901 Klyachkin All-Russia Business Directory, for Bessarabia and portions of Transnistria within the jurisdiction of the JewishGen Bessarabia Research Division. Records include grocers, haberdashers, manufacturers, bankers, and notaries working in the early 1900s who were apparently Jewish.

The information was published in the 1901 edition of the Klyachkin handbook, published in Warsaw by S. Klyachkin. The handbook covers more than 40,000 companies from 2,170 cities throughout the Russian Empire. The full directory is available as a PDF download (1,090 pages; images from page 459 onward are thumbnails requiring enlargement).

Note on place names: Place names have changed since 1901, sometimes on several occasions. The database shows the present name, county or province, and nation (Moldova or Ukraine) for each 1901 locality. Additional name history for each town is available via the JewishGen Communities Database.

Fields Recorded

Each record contains the following fields:

Name — Last Name + First Name (most often just an initial, if given)
Father — Father’s given name (just an initial), if given
Occupation (Russian) — Type of business, in Russian (Cyrillic alphabet)
Occupation (English) — Type of business translated: Banker, Grocer, Haberdasher, Manufacturer, or Notary
Size of Business — Small, Medium, or Large (if given)
Merchant’s Guild — I, II, or III (if given)
Town (1901) — Name of town in Russian, one of the 31 towns covered
Uyezd — The district as of 1901
Gubernia — The Russian province as of 1901: Bessarabia, Podolia, or Kherson
Modern Country — Moldova or Ukraine
Pages — Page number in the original directory
Comments — e.g., business partners or other information

Geo-Political History of the Region

The political status of Bessarabia and Transnistria changed multiple times across the 20th century, which affects how records are classified and where archives are held today:

  • Before WWI — Bessarabia and Transnistria were part of the Russian Empire. Bessarabia was its own guberniya; Transnistria was part of Podolia and Kherson guberniyas.
  • Between the World Wars — Bessarabia was part of Romania; Transnistria was part of the Soviet Union.
  • After WWII — Both regions were part of the Soviet Union.
  • After the Soviet breakup — The independent Republic of Moldova was formed from most of Bessarabia; significant portions came under Ukrainian rule.
  • 1990 — A portion of Transnistria (including Kamenka, Rybnitsa, Dubossary, Grigoriopol, and Tiraspol) and Bendery declared independence from Moldova as the “Pridnestrovian Moldavian Republic,” which has not been recognized by any UN member state.
