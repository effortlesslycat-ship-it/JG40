U.S. Consular Post, Bucharest — Emergency Passport Applications

942 Records

Immigration · Passport Applications · Romania

Background

This database consists of 941 extracts from the records of the U.S. Consular Post in Bucharest, Romania, 1860–1941, which are part of the U.S. National Archives’ Record Group 84 (RG 84). These records are stored at the U.S. National Archives II in College Park, Maryland.

Of the 138 volumes in RG 84 for Bucharest, eight were determined to contain significant genealogical material and were indexed. Volumes 14, 40, and 97 contain records of birth, marriage, death, and registration of American citizens (1879–1924). Volumes 134–138 contain emergency passports issued from January 1920 through September 1926, each application containing a photograph of the applicant and often other family members, along with naturalization, passport, and birth certificate numbers.

Sample documents: Several emergency passport applications from the collection are available. Each contains photographs, personal details, and often naturalization records. Click any image in the gallery below for a larger view.

Research Notes
Obtaining Original Records

Records can be requested by mail from: Archives II, Textual Reference Branch, 8601 Adelphi Road, College Park, Maryland 20740. Include: RG 84, MLR UD Number 195, Consulate location (Bucharest, Romania), Volume #, Page #, Date, Document #, and the complete name as it appears in the database.

For copies by mail, loose papers will be photocopied, but documents from bound volumes will be put on microfilm only. Minimum fee is $10 for the first 15 pages or fewer. On-site records are pulled five times daily, Monday through Friday.
