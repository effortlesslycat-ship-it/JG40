Chişinău Business Directory, 1940

1,278 Records

Business Directory · Bessarabia · Moldova

Background

This database contains nearly 1,300 entries of apparently Jewish names among government officials, private professionals, merchants, owners and operators of factories, shops, warehouses, and associations listed in the 1940 Chişinău commercial yearbook. They include accountants, agronomists, bakers, blacksmiths, dentists, doctors, jewelers, music teachers, and watchmakers, and sellers of groceries, furs, furniture, grain, and shoes — among nearly 150 distinct occupations.

Chişinău, in Romania between the two world wars, is now the capital city of Moldova. Prior to World War I it was known as Kishinev and was the capital of Bessarabia Gubernia in the Russian Empire.

The information was extracted from Anuarul Chişinăului pe anul 1940, a business and professional directory published in Romanian by “A R P I D.” Data for surnames that did not appear to be Jewish was omitted, with an attempt to err on the side of inclusion. A PDF of the original book (15 MB) is available for download.

Fields Recorded

Each record contains the following fields:

Name — Surname or firm name (Romanian); first name or initial if given
(English) — Surname in English pronunciation
Business — Occupation or profession (Romanian)
Type — Type of business (workshop, factory, store), translated to English
Address, Etc. — Address, telephone number, comments
Page in Book — Page number in the original volume
Page in PDF — Page number in the downloadable PDF
Research Notes
Missing Pages & Other Resources

The PDF is missing pages 95 through 99, which contain display advertising and individual listings for about half of the physicians. Copies of the missing pages were obtained separately to ensure a complete selection of Jewish doctors.

You can also search the contents of the complete yearbook on the Genealogy Indexer (maintained by Logan Kleinwaks), which is useful for surnames missing from this database. The search works best with Romanian spelling; try the OCR-Adjusted match option.

Searching This Database

This database is searchable via the JewishGen Romania Database and the JewishGen Ukraine Database.
