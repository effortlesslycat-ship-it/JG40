Bessarabia Duma Voters List

128,148 Records

Voter List · Bessarabia · Moldova · Ukraine

Background

This database contains the names of over 128,000 men of Bessarabia Gubernia (today in Moldova and Ukraine) who were eligible to vote in the Russian parliamentary elections of 1906 and 1907 (the 2nd and 3rd Dumas). Eligible voters were males over age 24 who also met other criteria — usually property or business ownership.

The lists were published in the Bessarabskie Guberniskie Vedomosti (the Russian provincial gazette), which is available on microfilm at the New York University Bobst Library and the Library of Congress [Microfilm (o) 2004/3002], with microfilms covering 1874–1917 on 34 reels.

The database includes both Jewish and Russian names — there was no designation by religion in the original lists. In the Orgeyev District, it is estimated that well over 50% of names are Jewish. The percentage of Jews in farming communities was lower, as Jews were predominantly in trades and were prohibited from owning land in many districts.

Important: Before searching, please read the Research Notes below. The transliteration method, name conventions, and known gaps in the data significantly affect how you should interpret your search results.

Fields Recorded

Each record contains the following fields:

Item — Identification allowing the team to locate the original entry
Name — Surname and given name
Patronym — Father’s given name
Qualification Reason — Why the person was eligible to vote (see Research Notes)
Town (Russian name) — Name as transliterated from the 1906–07 newspaper
Town (Modern name) — Current Moldovan or Ukrainian name
Uyezd — The Bessarabian district
Country — Moldova or Ukraine (today)
Year — 1906 (2nd Duma) or 1907 (3rd Duma)
Research Notes
Transliteration Method

The “Journalistic” transliteration table was used to convert Russian Cyrillic to Latin characters — the same table used by most newspapers and periodicals. This yields only plain letters (no accented characters). The key differences from other systems:

Cyrillic Letter Journalistic Library of Congress Beider
ЙYYJ
Ьnonenoneaccent
ЮYUIUYU
ЯYAIAYA

Names are entered exactly as transliterated, with no correction of obvious spelling or printing errors.

Name Conventions & What to Expect

Several conventions affect how names appear in this database:

  • The first name found was always treated as the surname.
  • The last name ending in -ov, -ev, -evich, or -ovich was treated as a patronym (“son of”), with exceptions for names where those endings are part of the name itself.
  • Some English letters have no Cyrillic equivalent and will never appear: standalone C, standalone H, J, Q, W, or X. Consequences include: Hersh → Gersh; Kohan → Kogan; Yakov for Jacob; Iosif for Joseph; Volf for Wolf.
  • The letters “p” and “n” were particularly difficult to distinguish in the microfilm. If you don’t find a name, try substituting one for the other.

We strongly recommend searching with Daitch-Mokotoff Soundex as well as the exact name.

Qualification Codes (Voter Eligibility)

Main city codes:

d. — Property owner (home, real estate, immovable property)
dg. — Property owner (value > 300 rubles)
kn. — Owns apartment (pays apartment tax)
kv. — Tenant (rents and pays apartment tax)
lpz. — Personal trade occupation (craftsman, tailor, etc.)
t. — Trade/Commerce (owns shop, merchant, dealer)
tp. — Trade/Industry (t. or factory owner)
tpl. — Pays business tax
ch. — Office worker (professional in government or public organization)
p. — Pensioner (receives government pension)
cr. — Clergyman
v. — Unspecified (one of the above)

Small village/farming community codes (values in desaytins = 2.7 acres, except d. which is in rubles):

l. — Landowner
lm. — Land Manager
lt. — Tenant/Lessee
c. — Clergyman
d. — Owns immovable property (value in rubles)

Proxy codes (a woman could authorize a male relative to vote for her):

(pdz.) — Authorized to vote for his wife
(pdo.) — Authorized to vote for his father
(pdm.) — Authorized to vote for his mother
** — Additional eligibility information available
Known Gaps in the Data

Two columns of data were missing from the original newspaper:

  • 2nd Duma, Orgeyev District (smaller towns): Names starting with D, E, I, Z, and part of K are missing. Approximately 100 names affected.
  • 2nd Duma, Vylkove (Ukraine): Names starting with S, T, U, and F are missing. Approximately 100 names affected.

Missing, questionable, or unreadable data is indicated as follows: “?” = totally unreadable; “Name?” = probably correct but uncertain; “–” = no name supplied; “Name1? / Name2?” = one of two possibilities; “Na…e” = middle letters unreadable.
