Bessarabia Out-Of-Town Jews

2,494 Records

Military Census · Bessarabia · Russian Empire

Background

In 1874–1875, the Russian Empire conducted a census of the male Jewish population known as the “Jewish Military Census.” This database contains census information about Jews who were registered in Bessarabia but who actually lived in other gubernias (provinces) of the Russian Empire, including Kherson, Podolia, Volyn, Grodno, Chernigov, and Tavricheskaya gubernias.

These “out-of-town” records are a unique genealogical resource, as they document Jewish families who had migrated away from their registered community but maintained their formal registration in Bessarabia. The original records are held at the National Archives of the Republic of Moldova (NARM).

Calendar note: Dates in this database are recorded according to the Julian calendar, which was used throughout the Russian Empire until 1918. To convert to the Gregorian (modern) calendar, add 12 days for dates between March 1800 and February 1900.
Fields Recorded

The Jewish Military Census records may contain the following information:

Page # — Page number of the image
Registration # — Number in order for each person in a town’s registry
Surname — Last name
Given Name — First name(s)
Father — Father’s given name (derived from the patronymic)
Relationship to Head of Household
Sex — M for male, F for female (only males in this dataset)
Age
Comments — Family status (Merchant, Middle Class, Farmer, Military), when registered, and sometimes origin information
Former Registration # — Registration number in a previous Revision (sometimes two prior numbers given)
Day, month, year of registration — Julian calendar
Uyezd — One of eight Bessarabian districts: Akkerman, Beltsy, Bendery, Izmail, Khotin, Kishinev, Orgeev, or Soroki
Gubernia — “Bessarabia”
Place of Residence — Town, Uezd, and Guberniya where the person actually lived
Place of Registration — Town, Uezd, and Guberniya where the person was formally registered
Type of Record — “Out-of-Town Jews”
Archive / Fond / Inventory / File — Archival source citation at NARM
Comments — Additional information of various types
Searching This Database

This database is searchable via the JewishGen Unified Search and the JewishGen Romania Collection.
