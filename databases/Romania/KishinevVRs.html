Vital Records · Bessarabia · Moldova · Ukraine

Background

This database contains Jewish birth, marriage, divorce, and death records from the former Russian Gubernia of Bessarabia. The original records are held at the National Archives of the Republic of Moldova (NARM) in Chişinău. They were microfilmed by the Genealogical Society of Utah and provided to JewishGen on CD-ROMs. These microfilms are also available through the LDS Family History Library and all LDS Family History Centers.

Records were originally created by the Jewish congregations of each town. The text is in both Russian (Cyrillic alphabet) and Hebrew script, though some records are in Russian only.

The vast majority of records are for the city of Kishinev (now Chişinău, Moldova), with additional records from Kaushany (Causeni), Beltsy (Balti), Bendery (Bender), Gancheshty (Hîncesti), Soroki (Soroca), Izmail (Izmayil, Ukraine), Novoselitsa (Novoselytsia, Ukraine), and smaller numbers from Chimishliya, Komrat, Manzyr, Petrovka, and Romanovka.

Complete record inventories with coverage by town and year are available via Google Sheets: Birth Records Inventory  ·  Marriage Records Inventory  ·  Divorce Records Inventory  ·  Death Records Inventory

Research Notes
Search Tips

Search these records using Beider-Morse Phonetic Matching (BMPM) and Daitch-Mokotoff Soundex (DM) as well as the exact name, due to spelling variations and because some records were transliterated from the Russian side only and not from the Hebrew side.

Search these records even if you know your relatives came from a different town. Researchers have found birth records for people born in Kishinev whose families were from as far as Grodno, Greece, and Holland. Our ancestors moved around considerably, so the record you are looking for could be in any database.
