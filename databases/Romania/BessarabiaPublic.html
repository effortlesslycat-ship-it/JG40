Jews in Public Life in Bessarabia

2,357 Records

Name List · Bessarabia · Moldova · Ukraine

Background

This database contains records of Jews listed in the annual Bessarabia Reference Calendar, Akkerman Calendar, and Kherson Calendar published by the Czarist government in the late 19th and early 20th centuries. These calendars, produced by the Governor’s office, contained reference materials on government organizations, religious organizations, charities, hospitals, schools, public associations, and trade associations, along with the names, ranks, titles, and positions of all ranking officials.

Information for this database was compiled from the personnel chapters of each calendar. Selections were made based on recognizably Jewish names or occupations (rabbi, Jewish school teacher, etc.). The current version contains records from Bessarabia Calendars for the years 1862 through 1914, as well as Akkerman and Kherson Calendar records transcribed in 2018.

Fields Recorded

Each record contains the following fields:

Name — Surname and given name(s)
Father — First name of the person’s father (if available)
Sex — M or F
Organization — Name of the organization the person belonged to
Department — Department or subsidiary (if available)
Occupation — Occupation as listed in the calendar for that year
Rank / Title — Social status, education, rank per Russian Table of Ranks, trade group, etc.
City — City of residence or occupation
Uyezd — District (Akkerman, Bendery, Izmail, Khotin, Kishinev, Orgeev, Soroki, or Yassi/Beltsi)
Gubernia — Always “Bessarabia”
Year — Year of publication
Source — Always “Calendar”
Page — Page number(s) in the calendar; multiple pages may be listed per record
Research Notes
Calendar Images

Photocopies (PDF files) of the original Bessarabia Calendars were received from the Boris Yeltsin Presidential Library. Images for all calendar years from 1849 through 1916 are available on the JewishGen Bessarabia Research Division website in the Current Research Projects section.

Sample Page

Page 51 of the Annual Calendar of 1887. The first half of the page lists Crown Rabbis in Kishinev, Khotin, Brichany, Akkerman, Orgeev, Beltsy, Skulyany, and Bendery.

Acknowledgements

This project of translating and transcribing the Bessarabia Reference Calendars began in January 2013, using electronic copies (PDF files) received from the Boris Yeltsin Presidential Library.

The information was reviewed, translated, and compiled by Yuriy Daylis, who dedicates his work to three generations of his ancestors — the Daylis, Rabinovitch, and Galperin families — who over the span of more than 125 years made a valuable contribution to the public and cultural life of Bessarabia and Moldavia.
