Pinkas from the Tomsk Jewish Community
By: Eric Feinstein

Background:

This is a transcription of a pinkas from the Tomsk Jewish community. It covers the deaths of the entire Tomsk uezd or district during the years 1859-1900.  There are approximately 2,500 entries.
The fields of information include:
  • Surname
  • Given Name
  • Father's Name
  • Some Spouse's Names
  • Christian Date of Death
  • Hebrew Date of Death
  • Place of Death
  • Place of Residence. 
  • There are also many notes about the deceased such a former soldier, husband or wife of resident of X village, etc. 

The original pages of this volume which is still located in Siberia, were photographed by the Center for Jewish Art, Hebrew University of Jerusalem as part of one of their Siberian expeditions to document Jewish life and art. 

Thank you to Vladimir Levin and Dr. Anna Berezin of the Center for Jewish Art, Hebrew University of Jerusalem at Mount Scopus, in Jerusalem for helping to arrange high resolution scans of all the pages to facilitate the transcription.  Thank you to Marina Smetanyuk for expertly transcribing everything and thank you to Jewish Generosity for sponsoring this important project.
