<H2>Introduction</H2>

<P>This database consists of 134 Jewish marriage records from Rzeszpw, Poland, from 1939-1942.

<p>German forces occupied Rzeszˇw on September 10, 1939. Before the occupation, there were some 14,000 Jews in Rzeszow, a total that increased as people were brought to the city from neighboring villages. The ghetto was established on December 17, 1941. Mass deportations of around 8,000 people took place between July 7 and 13, 1942, and were accompanied by mass executions outside the city, and random murders in ôactionsö within the city.

<A NAME="P2"></A>
<H3>Source</H3>
<p>This file is from the Rzeszˇw state archive, Fond 533, File 54. There are 134 records in this set.

<p>The last ten marriage records are from June 1942. This was shorty before the deportations to Belzec and mass murders in the town took place. As in other places in Poland where deportations had been expected, there was a hope that a marriage might secure a degree of protection for the woman, if the husband was employed and covered by work insurance. Very sadly, this turned out not to be the case.
