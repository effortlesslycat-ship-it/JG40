Background

The Germans occupied the city of Lublin almost immediately upon entering it on September 18, 1939.  During the occupation of the city, until the Lublin Ghetto was systematically liquidated beginning with deportation to Belzec Death Camp in March, 1942, the occupiers: registered the Jewish inhabitants; used them as forced labor; deported some families to other towns in a calculated attempt to manipulate the size of the Lublin Jewish population; and, kept records of the movement (travel) of Jews between Lublin and other towns and cities.

Beginning in September 1939 with the German invasion, people came to Lublin as refugees from smaller towns which had been heavily bombed and which lay on the path between Warsaw and Lublin.  The first wave of resettled refugees came from towns such as Kurów and Markuszów.  By December 1939, people were arriving in Lublin from Puławy.  The next large waves came from Sieradz, Kalisz, and eventually Łódź.  Many Jews from Łódź fled to Lublin because they were afraid of being enclosed in the Łódź Ghetto.  The next large wave of refugees fleeing to Lublin were from Kraków, and after that, it was mostly poorer Jews from smaller towns around the province.  Their eventual fate followed the fate of the native Lubliners.

This database is a directory of Lublin Jews who were officially signing in-and-out of Lublin at the Registration Office of the Lublin Judenrat between July 1, 1940 and January 31, 1941.  During this seven-month period, over 800 travel passes were issued from the Judenrat office.  Only about one dozen of the people traveling seem to have registered as both leaving from and arriving in Lublin during this period.  The overwhelming majority of these registered travelers have only one entry, whether it is leaving or arriving.  Only one person, Mr. Mordko Goldfarb, is shown with more than two passes, and his three passes record that he traveled from Warsaw, to Lublin, back to Warsaw, and finally back to Lublin, all during the month of June, 1940.  Through examples such as his, we can see that individual numbered passes were issued for each independent arrival or departure registered.

Some of the destinations or places where people were returning from included:

  • Arbeits-Lager = Labor Camp
  • Fluchtlingslager = Aligned Refugee Camp
  • Gefangenschaft = Captivity
  • Richtung unbek./Unbek.Richt. = Unknown Direction

On June 11, 1940 and June 17, 1940, there were large numbers of arrivals into Lublin of Jews coming from Łódź ("Litmannstadt" or "Lodsch", in the German spellings).  Łódź is the most frequently mentioned "other town" in this Lublin directory, and almost one quarter (193 out of 815) of the travel incidents involved Łódź at one end of the travel plans or the other.  The most commonly given address in Lublin is "Lubartowska 50" (including 50a and 50b), and most of the Łódź arrivals report Lubartowska 50 as their Lublin address.  According to Robert Kuwalek, a noted Lublin historian specializing in this time period, the register entries in this database document the flight of Łódź Jews to Lublin.

Upon arrival in Lublin, the Lublin Jewish community was faced with the task of finding accommodations for the unexpected arrivals.  Most were housed under terrible conditions in shops and attics around Lublin.  The synagogues and beit midrashes were filled with refugees, including the Eiger's synagogue at Szeroka 40.  The largest shelter was at the "Bengal Factory" on Lubartowska Street (probably the Lubartowska 50 address).  The Lubartowska shelter is described in the Judenrat files as highly primitive. and the Judenrat believed that it was the center of the Typhus epidemic that swept through the ghetto.

Database

There are 815 entries in this database of the Directory of Jews "Signing In and Out of Lublin" between July 1, 1940 and January 31, 1941.

The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Name (Surname + Given name)
  • Date of Birth / Age
  • Position on Page/List
  • Number
  • Registration Date (Aus. Datum)
  • Travel Permit Number (Aus. Karte)
  • Street Address
  • Travel Destination or Point of Origination
  • Status on List ("Traveling from" or "Traveling to" Lublin)
  • Comments
  • Image # (microfilm frame number, listed as a jpg file)

Acknowledgments

This directory of "Jews Signing In and Out of Lublin" can be found in the Lublin Judenrat files, which are housed at the Lublin branch of the Polish State Archives (in Polish: "Archiwów Państwowych w Lublinie").  The file is labeled "Sygnatura 152" of Zespolu 891/0 and appears on microfilm #118892 of the Polish State Archives branch in Lublin.  The U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum (USHMM) has a copy of all this material in Washington, D.C., and an additional copy exists at Yad Vashem in Jerusalem.

Freija Lindholm, a JewishGen volunteer, performed the data entry portion for this project, and it was proofread by Robinn Magid.
