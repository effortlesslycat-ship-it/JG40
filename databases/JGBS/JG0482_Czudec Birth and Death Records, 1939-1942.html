Introduction

This is database of 14 Jewish births and death records from Czudec, Poland from 1939-1942.

German forces occupied Czudec in September 1939. While Czudec was a small town, Jews from the surrounding region were subsequently brought into the town in significant numbers. There was never a ghetto in Czudec. Instead, Jewish men and women were hired out as forced labor to private or municipal enterprises - including those involved in construction, road building and estate management. Several of these enterprises were based in the nearby town of Strzyzów.

Source

The records in this database are from the Rzeszów State Archive, Fond 736 - File 16.

The fields in the database include:

  • Scan numbers (two columns)
  • URL link to scan containing record on Polish State Archive site
  • Fond/File number [736/16]
  • Record number
  • Town [Czudec]
  • Record type [B or D]
  • Given name(s)
  • Surname
  • Place of birth
  • Date of birth
  • Date of death
  • Age (years)
  • Marital status
  • Occupation (Polish or German)
  • Occupation (English)
  • Place of residence
  • Given name(s) of father
  • Given name(s) of father’s father
  • Father’s occupation
  • Father’s place of residence
  • Mother’s given name(s)
  • Mother’s surname
  • Given name of mother’s mother
  • Given name of mother’s father
  • Mother’s place of birth
  • Date of parents’ marriage
  • Place of parents’ marriage
  • Given name(s) of spouse
  • Note: Not all the fields are necessarily present in any given entry.
