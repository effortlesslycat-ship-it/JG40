Background

Cernăuţi (Romanian spelling) was in north-east Romania and was formerly the capital of Bukovina. In 1930 it had 46,000 Jewish inhabitants. When the Soviet Union took over the city in 1939, 10,000 Jews were deported to Siberia. The Germans took the city in 1941 and established a ghetto with 50,000 Jews. Deportations began to Transnistria in October-November 1941 and continued through June 1942, with about 33,000 Jews deported. 16,000 Jews remained in special occupations. After the war most of the survivors emigrated to Palestine / Israel.

This database encompasses a collection of small lists, all having to do with Jewish residents in Cernăuţi and the surrounding area. The lists cover forced labor, police reports, conversion lists, emigration, ghetto listings, and registration cards. Database

This database includes 3,968 records from the 61 lists detailed above. The fields for this database are as follows (Since information on each list varies, not all records will have information for each of the fields.):

  • List #
  • Number
  • Surname
  • Given Name
  • Place of Birth
  • Date of Birth
  • Age
  • Occupation (See note 2 below)
  • Street Address
  • City
  • Comments

Notes:

Note 1: When records indicated that individuals lived in family units, a search for any individual will return the other members of the household as well.

Note 2: Occupations have been entered from the source documents in Romanian. To assist the researcher with translations of the Romanian occupation entries, please see the JewishGen Info File at: http://www.jewishgen.org/infofiles/RomanianOccs.htm
