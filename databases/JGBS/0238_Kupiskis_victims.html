This database contains a list of 793 Holocaust victims from Kupiškis (Yiddish: קופּישאָק Kupishok), Lithiuania.

Background

A group comprised of thirteen descendants from the Meyerowitz, Goldin, Shapiro, Choritz, Gafanovich, and Sarfanovich families met in Kupiškis, Lithuania, on Wednesday, August 27, 1997.  This group was led by Norman and Alec Meyer, and was accompanied by Lithuanian guide Regina Kopelevich.  They were met on that Wednesday morning by the Mayor and city government of Kupiškis.  At the commencement of the meeting, the Mayor handed Norman Meyer a list of 808 Jews who were killed during the summer of 1941.  This list was compiled in 1946 by the midwife from the Jewish Maternity Hospital in Ponevezh (Panevėžys) and several other sources.

The Jews of Kupiškis were killed on several different occasions in various locations during the summer of 1941.  See Yad Vashem's Pages of Testimony for further details.  This list represents many of those residents of Kupiškis who were killed during that time.  As there are only 808 people listed out of approximately 1,200 known residents, a number of other people may have either escaped, been killed in other locations, or just been forgotten in the process of the listing of the names which occurred several years after the fact.

The list seemed to be created in family groupings or how the data entry people remembered the information.  Due to this, the list has not been reconstructed in alphabetical order, as it would have destroyed this organization.  The headings of the original columns are: "Vardas" (Last Name), Pavarde (First Name), and Amzius (Age).  (The original order can be seen on the JewishGen KehilaLinks site at http://kehilalinks.jewishgen.org/kupiskis/death2.htm).

When reading the surname, be aware that it contains the Lithuanian grammatical suffix endings, which reflect male, female, single and marrital status.  The "-ius" or "-as" is a married male (e.g.: Rabinovicius, Kaplanas), the "-iene" or "-iute" is a married female (e.g.: Rabinoviciene, Kaciute), and the "-aite" is an unmarried female (e.g.: Rabinovicaite).

In some cases, the person who entered the names spelled all the entries for a particular family with the same married masculine ending, as in "Joffe".  In that case, you will only be able to determine the gender of the individual by their first name, if it is given.

Where the information was not really known by the data entry person, a group notation was provided. such as "three children".  There were several double entries or entries which were added as an afterthought.  These are kept in the same manner as they were recorded on the list.  The scanned original pages of the list are to be found on the Yad Vashem Web Site, under Shoah-Related Lists.  Just plug in the word 'Kupiskis' for 'place' and then Details, and the pages will appear.  You can then increase their size so you can see them more clearly and also print them so as to look at them easily at your leisure.

For any further information, please see the JewishGen KehilaLinks site for Kupiškis, Lithuania, at: http://kehilalinks.jewishgen.org/kupiskis/kupishok.htm.

Database

This database includes 793 records of Holocaust victims from Kupiškis.  The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Name (Surname + Given name) —
    The nominative form of each surname is also given, in square brackets.
  • Number — the number in the order of the original list.
  • Age — (approximate).
  • Comments — (usually the relationship, if given).

Note: Translation of Relationships, Lithuanian to English:

  • Dukte = daughter
  • Motina = mother
  • Senelis = grandfather
  • Sunus = son
  • Tetule = aunt
  • Vykis = children
  • Vyras = husband
  • Zmona = wife
