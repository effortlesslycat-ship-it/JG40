Background

Brünnlitz, a sub-camp of Gross-Rosen, was established in 1944 just outside the town of Brněnec, Czechoslovakia.  It has become famous as the site of Schindler’s list.  This collection was prepared by Robert Monheit, a Jewish chaplain with the French army.  It is one of several versions of the list(s) of survivors, which vary slightly.  In many cases survivors ended up in displaced person camps, and it is possible to trace their later fate.

This list contains about 1,000 names, mostly of Polish Jews, but also contains persons/names from Slovenia, Czechoslovakia, Russia, France, Holland, Yugoslavia and Germany.  The information consists of family and given names, date of birth, and nationality.

Database

This database includes 990 survivors.  The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Surname
  • Given Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Nationality
