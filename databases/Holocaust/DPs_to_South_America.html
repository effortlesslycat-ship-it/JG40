List of Displaced Persons (DPs) Emigrating To South America - 1945

Introduction by YIVO Institute for Jewish Research

BACKGROUND

The Hebrew Immigrant Aid Society (HIAS) is the world’s oldest refugee agency, formally incorporated in 1903. HIAS emerged from the work of several agencies which supported Jews fleeing pogroms in Russia and Eastern Europe beginning in the 1880s.

This data set comes from records of HIAS’s work in France during the war years. HIAS provided assistance to refugees in the form of legal support, monetary aid, and family reunification services, among other things, during this timeframe.

DATABASE

This database includes 399 records of emigrants, leaving French ports, going to various destination.

The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Image # (Scan image number within the YIVO folder)       
  • Surname
  • Given name
  • Date of birth / Age
  • Place of birth
  • Nationality
  • Last residence
  • Occupation
  • Surname – relative/friend
  • Given name – relative / friend
  • Relationship – relative / friend
  • City – relative / friend
  • Destination
  • Comments

There are 6 different source document lists of emigrants.  Some of the pages are mixed, not in numerical order.

ACKNOWLEDGEMENTS

The information contained in this database was indexed from the files available from the Center for Jewish History’s collection of scanned source documents from the YIVO Institute for Jewish Research. This data set is from the “Records of HIAS-HICEM Main Office in Europe (RG 245.5, France I-IV)”, File: Box 86, Folder: France IV-4332, Reel: 19.36).  It can be accessed online at https://archives.cjh.org/repositories/7/archival_objects/176246
