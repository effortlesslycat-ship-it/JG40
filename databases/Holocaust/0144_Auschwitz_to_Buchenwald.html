Background

In the months preceding Soviet liberation of Auschwitz, tens of thousands of prisoners, Jews and non-Jews, were sent westward from Auschwitz to various camps in Germany. Some of them perished in transit, others in the new camps, but many survived. In any case, none of them died in Auschwitz, although many secondary sources continue to list them as perishing there.

This transport arrived in Buchenwald on January 22, 1945. The list, taken from the Captured German Records collection (A-3355 reel 133 at NARA and 1996 A-0342 reel 165 at the USHMM), is of poor legibility. Nevertheless, it was possible to decipher almost all the names. Blank spaces were left where the material was illegible. The information was entered as it appeared, i.e. categories of prisoners, personal names and town names were entered as they appeared. The last column, Auschwitz prisoner number, was particularly difficult to read, and since it is impossible to confirm these numbers, they should be used with caution.

The fate of these persons once they arrived in Buchenwald is not given. Researchers seeking such information on individuals should contact the Buchenwald Gedenkstätte (D-99427 Weimar, Germany, email buchenwald@buchenwald.de, or fax 49 3643 43010.).

Database

This database includes 2,207 records.

The fields of the database are as follows:

  • Surname
  • Given Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Place of Birth
  • Prisoner Number
  • Profession
  • Auschwitz Prisoner Number
  • Prisoner Category
