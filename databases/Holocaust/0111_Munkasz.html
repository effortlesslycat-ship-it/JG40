Background

At the end of 1919, Hungary established the Munkaszolgįlat (Labor Service) for civilians who were either unwilling or unable to serve in the regular army.  This institution was unique to Hungary and was not originally directed at Jews.  In 1938, however, the Labor Service was transformed into a forced labor institution directed solely at Jews.  "Munkaszolgįlatos" were Jews who served in the Munkaszolgįlat.  As the war progressed, the situation of the Jews in these labor companies became extremely difficult, and many did not survive.  More detailed information on the history of the Munkaszolgįlat may be found at http://english.gfh.org.il/hungary2.htm.

The Database

The database includes 4,497 records.  This database, the Jewish KMSZ / Jewish Katonai Munkaszolgalat / military forced laborer list, is a list of Jews who died while serving in the Munkaszolgįlat during World War II.  Zichronam l'Vracha — May their memory be a blessing.

The fields of the database are as follows:

  • Surname, Given Name
  • Mother's Surname, Mother's Given Name
  • Date of Birth
  • Place of Birth

Note: These people are also listed on the following website http://www.hadifogoly.hu, which is in Hungarian.  Next to "Vezetéknév" (Surname), enter the surname you are looking for.
