Background

This file contains the names of 1,799 survivors found on liberation in the hospitals at Auschwitz, Birkennau and Monowitz on January 27, 1945, as compiled by the Polish Red Cross.

Auschwitz was liberated on January 27th, 1945 by the Russian Army.  When the Russians liberated Auschwitz they found a large number of prisoners in the hospital.  The complete name of the collection is "Register of persons found in Auschwitz, Birkenau, and Monowitz after the liberation. Some of them died after on (marked with a cross). The register was complied at Auschwitz by the Polish Red Cross Camp's Hospital, and started in February 1945.

Starting on February 7, 1945, i.e. within a week after the liberation of Auschwitz, transports took the surviving prisoners away from the camp. For some of them, the date of the transport is mentioned in the list.  Other prisoners could not be saved and died after the liberation. In some cases their deaths are marked in Polish or German, and in some cases their deaths are marked with a "+". In those cases a note will be found in the comments column "+ (marked as having died)". 

The source list was compiled by the Polish Red Cross and includes persons who perished after liberation.  It is not completely clear who created the original list since there is an inconsistency with the language used for "Nationality" entries.  For example, in some cases, the entry could be Wegry (Hungary in Polish) and in other cases Ung. (Hungary in German).

It is noteworthy that there are quite a few survivors who are described as "Austrians" at a time when the Austrian territory had not even been liberated (Vienna was only liberated mid-April 1945) or been recreated.

Creating the list appears to have come from registration cards from the Auschwitz administrators.  Those prisoners for whom no record could be found they were marked as "ohne Karteikarte" (without registration card). 

Database

This database includes 1,799 survivors found on liberation in the hospitals at Auschwitz, Birkennau and Monowitz.  The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Page #
  • Surname
  • Given Name
  • Prisoner #
  • Nationality
  • Nationality translated in to English
  • Date of Birth
  • Age
  • Date of Death
  • Comments

Acknowledgments

The information contained in this database was indexed from the files available at the United States Holocaust Memorial Museum.  The original source of the material was within the holdings of the International Tracing Service, Arolsen, Germany). Kurt Friedlaender, a JewishGen volunteer, compiled the list and Fritz Neubauer proofread and commented on it.
