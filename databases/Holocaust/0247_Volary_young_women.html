Background

This database includes the names of 85 Jewish women who died in Volary, Czechoslovakia, as well as 154 who were brought to medical facilities when United States troops arrived.

As the Russians approached from the East, there were mass evacuations, including forced marches of Jews and other prisoners from various camps.  The marches included women who had been held as forced laborers in various Gross-Rosen camps.  One of the places to which women prisoners were forced to march was Volary (Wallern, at 48°54'N 13°53'E), in South Bohemia, Czechoslovakia.  Given the nature of these forced marches and lack of food and shelter, many of the prisoners died.

This collection includes the names of 85 Jewish women (List I), mostly from Poland and Hungary, who died in Volary, as well as 154 (List II) who were brought to medical facilities when United States troops arrived.  Unfortunately, some of those that were treated at the facility did not survive, and are also on the list of those that died.

The source of this information is material from the U.S. National Archives and Records Administration (NARA), held in a file at the U.S. Holocaust Memorial Museum (USHMM), VC0106.

Database

This database includes 239 records from two lists from Volary.  The fields for this database are as follows:

  • Name (Surname + Given Name)
  • Date of Birth
  • Age
  • Place of Birth - City + Country (List I only)
  • Last residence - City + Country (List II only)
  • Place of Death
  • Date of Death
  • Comments
  • Source (List I or List II)
  • Burial Location (List I only)
