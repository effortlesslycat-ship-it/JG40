The Jews of Courland: A Basic Guide to Using LDS Sources

The following microfilms in the collection of the LDS Family History Library can be ordered through their international network of Family History Centres and are available on an open shelf basis in Salt Lake City. The following rolls contain material identified as "Hebräer" or Jewish by the Baltic Germans when they filmed the material in the Riga archives in 1940/41. In fact many additional rolls which are currently being inventoried have previously unidentified Jewish material.

The following LDS microfilms have documents relating to the following cities and towns that have specifically Jewish interest.

Tips:

  • Take a print out of this Guide with you. It acts as a map to each roll. Without it the journey can be very confusing.
  • Find all the parts to the list you are interesting in searching, i.e. in the case of Hasenpoth, Roll 1344282 the list number is 15. List 15 is split into two halves. You are likely to need to search both sections of the list.
  • Remember to roll the film in the same direction. The images can be in reverse order and upside down you need to be sure you are advancing through the roll. It is surprisingly easy to find you are going backwards and not realise it.
  • Don't let these instructions put you off. You will get the hang of it as you gain experience and there is a real treasure chest of documentation to be viewed.
  • Enjoy
