Bobov Yeshiva Interwar Student List

825 RECORDS

School/Student List · Poland

Background

This is a list of some of the students who learned in the Etz Chaim network of Yeshivas in Poland during the interwar 1918-1939 period. This was the educational system of the Bobov Chassidic movement. The original Yeshiva of the Bobover Rebbes was located in the Galician town of Bobov itself. However, during the time of the Grand Rabbi Ben Zion Halberstam, author of Kedushas Tzion., the influence of the Bobov dynasty was strengthened and ultimately as many as sixty separate branches of the yeshiva under the name Etz Chaim were established throughout Galicia.

The names of the students or Talmidim were published in a work about the life and work about Rabbi Ben Zion Halberstam, a Hebrew book [Sefer] called Arzei Levanon [Cedars of Lebanon] Volume 3, "MiTzion Tezei Torah."

Acknowledgments

The information contained in this database was transcribed by Malka Chosenek and Laurie Margolies. It was donated by Jacob Rosen-Koenigsbuch, the former Israeli ambassador to Egypt and Jordan. He is also a distinguished genealogist who has compiled vital Jewish surname databases for many places in the Arab World.

Fields Recorded

This database includes 825 names of students from Poland. It is not clear from the book [Sefer] when the students attended the Etz Chaim Yeshivas and in what year the list was compiled. The original list was alphabetically organized according to given name.

The fields in the database are:

Name
Age
Father's name
Comments (For a few individuals it lists the mother's name here)
