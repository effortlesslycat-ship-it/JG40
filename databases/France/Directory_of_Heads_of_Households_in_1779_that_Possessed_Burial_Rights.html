Directory of Head of Households in 1779 that Possessed Burial Rights

Introduction by: Eric Feinstein

BACKGROUND

The Jewish cemetery in Jungholz / Jungholtz in Alsace served as a community cemetery for the region around it starting in the mid-1600s. The historian and Rabbi, Moise Ginsburger published a comprehensive history of the cemetery in 1904. Der Israelitische Friedhof in Jungholz. Verlag: Eugène Barth, Guebwiller, 1904. It was also published by the publishers C.Ehrmann in Mulhouse in 1904. As an appendix to this history of the cemetery Rabbi Ginsburger included a list of those Jewish heads of households with burial rights in the cemetery in 1779. The original list from 1779 was compiled before Jewish people assumed permanent family surnames. The list was then updated to include the last names that were taken on in 1784. The numbers in each entry, where applicable, also refer to the 1784 Census of Alsace and correspond to the Jewish family number in each town.

The towns included in this list are Bollweiler, Dornach, Froeningen, Foussemagne, Gebweiler, Habsheim, Hagenbach, Hartmannsweiler, Hattstatt, Häusseren, Horburg, Ingersheim, Isenheim, Jungholz, Lumschweiler, Pfastatt, Regisheim, Riedweier, Rixheim, Sennheim, Staffelfelden, Steinbrunn, Sulz, Sulzmatt, Thann, Türkheim, Uffholz, Vögtlinshofen, Wattweiler, Wettolsheim, Wittelsheim, Wittenheim Wintzenheim, Zillisheim, and Zimmersheim.

DATABASE

There are 570 entries in this database. This database includes the fields: Town, Surname, Given Name, Father’s Name, Number (House number according to the 1784 Alsace Census) and Comments.
