Foreign Jews in the Regional Archives of Burgundy

Introduction by: Eric Feinstein

BACKGROUND

Pierre-Jacques Derainne is one the leading academic scholars who has documented immigration to France. In 2008 he published his Histoire et mémoire des immigrations en région Bourgogne [The history and memory of immigration in the Burgundy region] It was published by the Maison des Sciences de l’Homme Dijon (UMS CNRS-uB 2739) Université de Bourgogne Pôle d’économie et de gestion BP 26 611 21 066 DIJON cedex. The work contains an extensive archival bibliography which indexed all the files pertaining to foreigners in the departmental and communal archives. Based on that work, an index has been made of all the individuals described as Jewish, either “juif” or “Israelite.” Additional research was done on a few of the names that were demonstrably Jewish to prove that they were in fact Jewish and were thus incorporated in this database. One list is included below for reference as it did not have any surnames attached to it in order to warrant inclusion on the database list but for obvious reasons is of general interest. It is the archived list of Jews arrested in Dijon and deported in 1944.

W 21058/2699 Liste des juifs arrêtés à Dijon et déportés en 1944, 1947. 1945-1971.

DATABASE

This database includes 186 foreign Jews (men and women) whose families lived in the Burgundy region during the early 20th Century.

The fields in the database are: Surname, Given Name, Archival Source and Comments. The language of all the information in the comments section is French.
