Michalovce (Nagy-Mihály) District

The database contains information about 811 Jews living in the Michalovce District (today in Slovakia), recorded in the 1930 Census of Czechoslovakia.

The second Czechoslovak population census was conducted on 1 December 1930.  (The first Czech census was in 1921).  The statistical results of the 1930 Census were published in eight volumes, with a separate volume for housing data.  This Census enumerated 136,737 Jews in Slovakia, out of a total Jewish population of 356,830 in all of Czechoslovakia.  Slovakia’s Jewish population represented 4.11 percent of the total population of Slovakia.

The second census recorded 3,386 persons who identified themselves as Jews in the Michalovce district.  The Michalovce district of inter-war Czechoslovakia was previously the Nagy-Mihály járás of Zemplén megye of Hungary before WWI, and today is part of Košický kraj (the Košice Region) in south-east Slovakia.

This database includes 29 persons in Zbudza (formerly Izbugya), 25 in Voľa (Laborcz-Volya), as well as 757 in the city of Michalovce (Nagymihály), the largest place in the district.

Database Fields

The fields displayed in this database are:

  • Town: Place of Residence, as listed in record.
  • Town (Hungarian): Former Hungarian name of the Place of Residence.
  • House #: A number assigned by the census taker to the household.
  • Surname: Surname of the individual.  Surnames of females add the suffix "-OVA" to the surname of the husband, or to the surname of the father for unmarried women.
  • Given Name: Given Name(s), spelled as listed in record.
  • Year Born: Year of birth, as listed in record.
  • Town Born: Town or village of birth, as listed in record.
  • District / Region of Birth: The district or region where birth place is located, if this information was provided.

Acknowledgements

This set of records was provided to the JewishGen Hungarian SIG by an anonymous donor.  The original records are in the Slovak State Archives [Slovenský národný archív] in Bratislava, Slovakia.
