Pester Lloyd
Introduction by: E. Randol Schoenberg

Background:

The Pester Lloyd was a German-language newspaper in Budapest from 1854 to 1945.  Over the years it was prominent enough to attract such internationally famous contributors as Theodor Herzl, Max Nordau, Thomas Mann, Stefan Zweig, Joseph Roth, Alfred Polgar, Ferenc Molnár, Dezső Kosztolányi, Egon Erwin Kisch, Bertha von Suttner, Franz Werfel and Felix Salten.  Jews in Budapest frequently posted obituary notices in the Pester Lloyd.  

This database of 5,821 obituary notices was compiled by Georg Gaugusch.  Issues of the Pester Lloyd are viewable online from https://anno.onb.ac.at/cgi-content/anno?aid=pel
