Aufbau Family Notices, 1940 - 2004

General Background

Aufbau was first published in New York in 1934 as a weekly newspaper for German-Jewish exiles. The newspaper provided not only coverage of the day’s major events for German Jews but also let the exiled community place announcements of important life cycle events. If a German-Jewish family wanted to let their former neighbors, friends, and family scattered around the globe know about a birth or death, they were sure to place the notice in Aufbau. For this reason the family notices placed in Aufbau are a vital resource for researchers wanting to discover what became of family members after they emigrated from Germany. Each entry in the database contains the individual’s name, the date and type of event, the place of origin in Germany and the date of the issue in which the announcement appeared. The project to index these notices was first started in the late 1990s by Harry Katzman and for many years was the result of the work of dozens of volunteers.

Project Status

There are about 95,000 notices indexed in these databases. More than 90% of the family notices have been indexed and work is underway to complete the remaining issues. The largest remaining gaps are: 1942 (June, November) / 1943 (March, April, September, October) / 1947 (January, February, August, September, November) / 1949 (January - March, August - December) / 1950 (September - December) / 1953 (September - December) / 1975 (July - October) / 1979 (July, August) / 1980 (January - March) / 1987 (September - December) / 1988 (June, July) /1995 (all) / 2002 - 2004.

How to Use the Database

The database is split into two components, which each contain different year ranges. Notices indexed prior to 2023 (the "legacy database") will appear in search results under "Aufbau Family Notices: 1940 - 1951, 1960 , 1974 - 2003." Notices that were indexed in 2023 and 2024 can be found in: "Aufbau Family Notices: 1952 - 1959, 1961- 1973." The second database contains direct links to a digitized version of the newspaper page where the notice appears. Aufbau was digitized by the Leo Baeck Institute and is hosted on the Internet Archive.

To find a copy of the notice appearing in the legacy database, click on the link in the last column of the search results, which will take you a folder for that year. Pages containing the notices will be in the folder, usually in chronological order. If you see something like "j10a43" that is a reference to the year and issue of publication. You will generally find 1 to 3 pages in the folder with that code, and one of them should have the notice you are looking for. You can also locate the issue manually at http://www.archive.org/details/aufbau/.

Searching the Database

The Aufbau Family Notices databases can be searched via the JewishGen Germany Database and the JewishGen Unified Search.
