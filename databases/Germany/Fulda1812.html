Fulda Department Register of Jewish Males 1812

Background

The Fulda-Departement Register of Jewish Males covers a part of the Napoleonic Kingdom of Westphalia, which ordered surname adoption for the region in 1808 and again in 1812. The region includes Kassel and northern Hessen, plus the eastern part of what became Westphalia later on. It does not include Fulda; the name refers to the river, not the town. These records are the foundational surname-adoption document for the region and the reason why Prussia never published a list of surname adoptions in the 1840s there when the rest of Westphalia did.

These records list the parents, which can get researchers back to 1750 or before in most cases and shows that a lot of the Jews in the region had surnames before then, too. Wives are mentioned only if they have a living son.
