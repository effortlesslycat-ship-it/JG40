Planned Giving

A planned gift creates a meaningful personal legacy at JewishGen, while securing the future of this important organization and its mission of preserving Jewish history for generations to come.

Contribute to the future of JewishGen by planning for a special kind of gift. There are many ways to include JewishGen in your charitable giving and estate planning — choose the path that best fits your goals.

Ways to Give

Bequest

One of the easiest and most important ways to support JewishGen's future is by naming JewishGen as a beneficiary in your will. A bequest may be made with a specific dollar amount, asset, or percentage of your estate. Please let us know if you have made a provision for JewishGen in your estate plans, so that we may honor you on JewishGen's Tree of Life Society.

Submit a Letter of Intent ›

Retirement Plans & Beneficiary Designations

Retirement (e.g., IRA), life insurance, and real estate assets may all be donated to JewishGen. Making a gift in this manner can mean significant tax advantages for your estate and your heirs.

Recognition

Tree of Life Society

Donors who contribute through a planned gift — or notify us that they have made a provision for JewishGen in their estate plans — are honored and recognized as members of JewishGen's Tree of Life Society. Together with your own financial planners, we can help you create a legacy that meets your unique financial, tax, and estate planning goals while ensuring JewishGen's mission of preserving our history for future generations continues.
