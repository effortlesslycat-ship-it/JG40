About the Division

The JewishGen Sephardic Research Division is dedicated to preserving the rich heritage of Sephardic Jews worldwide. Because our history is not geographically constrained, we collect and exchange resources for communities wherever they took root in the global Sephardic diaspora.

Our purview includes the Portuguese or Western Sephardim (primarily in Amsterdam, Bordeaux, Bayonne, Hamburg, Livorno, and the New World), as well as the Eastern Sephardim from the Ottoman Empire and Morocco. To offer a comprehensive view of our shared history, we also document deeply intertwined communities, including the Romaniotim, Italkim, Mughrabim, Mustarabim, Parsim, and Temanim.

If you would be interested in making a major contribution to the Sephardic Research Division's efforts, please email Development@mjhnyc.org.

Currently Working On

The Sephardic Research Division is currently working on:

  • The Ottoman Registers Project
  • Salonika Declarations
  • Obtaining, translating, and indexing records from the Greek Archives
  • Records from the Portuguese Communities (Amsterdam, Hamburg, Livorno, Pisa)
  • Translation and indexing of the 1899 Tehran Census
