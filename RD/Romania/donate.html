About the Division

Holding more than 1.2 million records, the JewishGen Romania Research Division preserves and shares the rich history of Jewish life in historic Romania, including Moldova, and historic Bessarabia through archival records, vital records, and specialized research initiatives. The Division documents life in small shtetls and outlying communities, offering a comprehensive view of social, economic, and cultural life across centuries, including census data, voter lists, business directories, vital records, and records of Holocaust survival and Soviet-era persecution.

Donor support enables the Division to acquire new materials, index rare records, and make them widely accessible for future generations.
